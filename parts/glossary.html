Latin Mass Glossary

A quick vocabulary guide for the most common words, actions, and structures in the traditional liturgy.

Open-Licensed Manuscripts and Photos

Ordo Missae

The fixed structure of the Mass, distinct from the changing seasonal propers.

Proper

Texts that change by day or feast, such as the Introit, Collect, and Communion verse.

Canon

The central Eucharistic prayer of the Mass, culminating in consecration.

Consecration

The sacramental moment when bread and wine become the Body and Blood of Christ.

Sanctus Bell

A bell rung to signal sacred moments and gather attention for adoration.

Asperges

A rite of sprinkling with holy water, often before Sunday Mass, signifying purification.

Genuflection

Bending one knee in adoration, especially in reverence before the Blessed Sacrament.

Ite, missa est

The dismissal, sending the faithful forth to live what they have received.
