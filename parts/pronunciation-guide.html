Ecclesiastical Latin Pronunciation Guide

These are church-Latin rules commonly used in the Roman liturgy. Use this page to practice core responses before or after Mass.

Audio references: A1 to A6.

Core Sound Rules

Vowels

a as in father

e as in they (shorter)

i as in machine

o as in for

u as in rule

Consonants

c + e/i/ae/oe sounds like ch (cae-li = CHEH-lee)

g + e/i/ae/oe sounds like j (re-gina = reh-JEE-nah)

gn sounds like ny (a-gnus = AHN-yoos)

ti + vowel often sounds like tsee (gra-ti-as = GRAHT-see-ahs)

Common Endings

-us usually "oos" (Do-mi-nus)

-ae sounds like "eh" (mae = meh)

-oe sounds like "eh" (coe-li = CHEH-lee)

h is usually silent

Stress Pattern

In many 3+ syllable words, stress the second-to-last syllable if it is long/heavy; otherwise stress the third-to-last.

Examples: Do-MI-nus, spi-RI-tu, mi-se-RE-re.

Practice Responses

Et cum spiritu tuo

Syllables: et koom SPEE-ree-too TOO-oh

Meaning: And with thy spirit.

Dominus vobiscum

Syllables: DOH-mee-noos voh-BEE-skoom

Meaning: The Lord be with you.

Agnus Dei

Syllables: AHN-yoos DEH-ee

Meaning: Lamb of God.

Domine non sum dignus

Syllables: DOH-mee-neh non soom DEEN-yoos

Meaning: Lord, I am not worthy.

Sanctus, Sanctus, Sanctus

Syllables: SAHNK-toos SAHNK-toos SAHNK-toos

Meaning: Holy, Holy, Holy.

Ite, missa est

Syllables: EE-teh MEE-sah est

Meaning: Go, the Mass is ended.
