Propers by Season

Advent, Lent, Easter, and Saints-Day Samples

This section adds representative propers to complement the fixed Order of Mass. Each entry includes core Latin text with facing English.

Source notes: S1, S4, S5.

Advent I (First Sunday of Advent)

Seasonal Proper

Latin

Introit: Ad te levavi animam meam: Deus meus, in te confido, non erubescam.

Collect: Excita, quaesumus, Domine, potentiam tuam, et veni.

Epistle incipit: Fratres, scientes quia hora est jam nos de somno surgere.

Gospel incipit: In illo tempore: Dixit Jesus discipulis suis: Erunt signa in sole, et luna, et stellis.

English

Introit: To Thee have I lifted up my soul: O my God, in Thee I trust, let me not be confounded.

Collect: Stir up Thy power, we beseech Thee, O Lord, and come.

Epistle incipit: Brethren, knowing that it is now the hour for us to rise from sleep.

Gospel incipit: At that time Jesus said to His disciples: There shall be signs in the sun, and in the moon, and in the stars.

Advent starts the liturgical year with expectation, vigilance, and prayer for deliverance.

Lent I (First Sunday of Lent)

Seasonal Proper

Latin

Introit: Invocabit me, et ego exaudiam eum: eripiam eum, et glorificabo eum.

Collect: Deus, qui Ecclesiam tuam annua Quadragesimali observatione purificas.

Epistle incipit: Fratres, exhortamur vos ne in vacuum gratiam Dei recipiatis.

Gospel incipit: In illo tempore: Ductus est Jesus in desertum a Spiritu, ut tentaretur a diabolo.

English

Introit: He shall call upon Me, and I will hear him: I will deliver him and glorify him.

Collect: O God, who dost purify Thy Church by the yearly observance of Lent.

Epistle incipit: Brethren, we exhort you that you receive not the grace of God in vain.

Gospel incipit: At that time Jesus was led by the Spirit into the desert, to be tempted by the devil.

Lent frames spiritual combat through fasting, repentance, and dependence on grace.

Easter Sunday

Seasonal Proper

Latin

Introit: Resurrexi, et adhuc tecum sum, alleluia: posuisti super me manum tuam, alleluia.

Collect: Deus, qui hodierna die per Unigenitum tuum aeternitatis nobis aditum devicta morte reserasti.

Epistle incipit: Fratres, expurgate vetus fermentum, ut sitis nova conspersio.

Gospel incipit: In illo tempore: Maria Magdalene et altera Maria venerunt videre sepulchrum.

English

Introit: I arose and am still with Thee, alleluia: Thou hast laid Thy hand upon me, alleluia.

Collect: O God, who on this day by Thy only-begotten Son hast opened for us the gate of eternity by conquering death.

Epistle incipit: Brethren, purge out the old leaven, that you may be a new dough.

Gospel incipit: At that time Mary Magdalene and the other Mary came to see the sepulchre.

Easter proclaims Christ's victory over death and the opening of eternal life for the Church.

Saint Joseph (19 March)

Saints-Day Proper

Latin

Introit: Justus ut palma florebit: sicut cedrus Libani multiplicabitur.

Collect: Sanctissimae Genetricis tuae Sponsi, quaesumus, Domine, meritis adjuvemur.

Epistle incipit: Ecce sacerdos magnus, qui in diebus suis placuit Deo.

Gospel incipit: Jacob autem genuit Joseph virum Mariae, de qua natus est Jesus.

English

Introit: The just man shall flourish like the palm tree: he shall grow like the cedar of Lebanon.

Collect: We beseech Thee, O Lord, that we may be helped by the merits of the spouse of Thy most holy Mother.

Epistle incipit: Behold a great priest, who in his days pleased God.

Gospel incipit: Jacob begot Joseph, the husband of Mary, of whom was born Jesus.

Saint Joseph's proper highlights justice, hidden fidelity, and guardianship of the Incarnate Word.

All Saints (1 November)

Saints-Day Proper

Latin

Introit: Gaudeamus omnes in Domino, diem festum celebrantes sub honore omnium Sanctorum.

Collect: Omnipotens sempiterne Deus, qui nos omnium Sanctorum tuorum merita sub una tribuisti celebritate venerari.

Epistle incipit: Vidi alterum angelum ascendentem ab ortu solis.

Gospel incipit: In illo tempore: Videns Jesus turbas, ascendit in montem.

English

Introit: Let us all rejoice in the Lord, celebrating a festival day in honor of all the Saints.

Collect: Almighty eternal God, who hast granted us to venerate the merits of all Thy Saints in one solemn feast.

Epistle incipit: I saw another angel ascending from the rising of the sun.

Gospel incipit: At that time, Jesus seeing the multitudes, went up into a mountain.

This feast manifests the communion of saints and the Church's final destiny in heavenly glory.
